## Minor Findings

### 1. `questions.1-2-1.items[3]` — Altitude Definition Framing Mismatch

The stem asks what "altitude" means "in this context" of "4,500 feet altitude,"
but the correct answer is the full general definition (indicated altitude MSL,
flight level, or both) rather than the context-appropriate meaning (MSL). The
explanation acknowledges the disconnect but the stem-to-answer framing is
mismatched. **Rephrase the stem to ask for the general definition without the
context qualifier.**

### 2. `flashcards.1-1-12.items[0]` — AJI Email: Duplicates Existing Card

This card tests a single email address (`9-AJI-SMS@faa.gov`) as a retrieval
target. The existing flashcard "Three ways to obtain ATO SMS info" already
covers this email alongside the SMS Toolbox and Quality Control Group as a
unified channel set. The staged card extracts one element and tests it in
isolation. **Drop or repurpose to test a different 1-1-12 concept not yet in
flashcard format.**

### 3. `flashcards.1-2-4.items[0]` — Reference Function: Duplicates Existing Card

The front and back are nearly identical to the existing "What do references do
in this order?" flashcard. The staged version adds enumerated source examples
and a notes comparison, but the core retrieval target is the same definitional
fact. **Replace with a contrast card (references vs. notes) or another 1-2-4
angle.**

### 4. `activities.1-1-12.items[0]` — SMS Dual Duty: Overlaps Existing Activity

The scenario (coworker claims SMS belongs only to the safety office) and
correct answer overlap substantially with the existing situation_action for
1-1-12. Both items use the same narrative device (coworker misconception)
and reach the same operational conclusion. **Drop or re-scaffold around a
different operational situation, such as SMS training access eligibility.**

## Suggestion-Level Findings

### 5. `flashcards.1-2-5.items[0]` — Change Page Markings: Low Retrieval Value

Testing document-production conventions (change number + effective date on
revised pages) is better suited to reference lookup than active flashcard
recall. **Consider replacing with a higher-value 1-2-5 concept.**

### 6. `flashcards.1-2-5.items[1]` — Unchanged Page Reprints: Low Retrieval Value

Same concern — testing that unchanged pages are reprinted in original form
is document-production trivia. **Same recommendation as #5.**

### 7. `flashcards.1-1-8.items[0]` — OPR Address: Excessive Retrieval Scope

The back includes the full street address and ZIP code. Knowing AJV-P is the
OPR is a legitimate reference fact, but memorizing the physical address is
excessive for active recall. **Tighten the back to emphasize AJV-P and its
role, keeping the address as supplementary context.**

## Patterns Observed

### Remediation Compliance

The staged batch shows strong adherence to the prior remediation guidance:

- **No paragraph-location scaffolding.** Every item uses self-contained
  operational framing (scenario-based stems, "According to FAA Order JO
  7110.65…") — no "Under 1-1-XX" prefixes appear anywhere.
- **Well-randomized answer positions.** Correct answers appear in positions
  1 through 4 across the item set with no systematic bias.
- **Plausible distractors.** Distractors represent realistic operational
  mistakes — confusing AJT/DoD/non-FAA routing paths, assuming "Going Tactical"
  equals "Due Regard," conflating notes with references.
- **No answer-length cues.** All distractors are comparable in length to the
  correct answer within each item.
- **Quality explanations.** Explanations cite the governing paragraph, correct
  the strongest misconception, and provide operational rationale.
